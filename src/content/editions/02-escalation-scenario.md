  <p>The rupee appears stable at 279, but the real story is the REER climbing to 105.17 — a 7.5-year high. In real, purchasing-power terms the rupee is overvalued, which quietly erodes export competitiveness and raises the real cost of the import bill even as the nominal rate holds.</p>

  <div class="callout">
    <strong>Why this matters</strong>
    Nominal stability is masking a structural imbalance. Exporters feel the squeeze first; importers feel relief that won't last if the rupee corrects.
  </div>

<!-- more -->

  <p>The IMF completed Pakistan's third EFF review and disbursed $1.3B to the SBP, lifting reserves to $17.1B. But that buffer is being consumed by a rising oil import bill and a current account slipping back into deficit.</p>
